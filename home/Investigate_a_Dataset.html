Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

The dataset chosen to be analysed is the No-Show Appointments database. This dataset gathers data from over 100,000 medical visits in Brazil and is concerned with the issue of whether or not patients arrive for their scheduled appointment. Each row has several features pertaining to the patient. The folowing are the columns provided in the original data: patient ID, appointment ID, gender, scheduled day, appointment day, age, neighbourhood, scholarship, ailments (hypertension, diabetes, alcoholism, and handicap), sms received, and no show.

The significance of the columns provided reveals the relationship between the availability and the level of committments patients (males and females) have towards their medical appointments in some areas in Brazil. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row: ● ‘Scheduled Day’ tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment. ● ‘Neighborhood’ indicates the location of the hospital. ● ‘Scholarship’ indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Família. ● No show column: ‘No’ if the patient showed up to their appointment, and ‘Yes’ if they did not show up.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

1.) What is the percentage of male to female who showed up for appointments?

2.) What is the percentage of patients with hypertension?

3.) Which age group attended the appointment the most?

# Creating a function that will be reused.
def piePlot(data,labels,colors,explode,title):
    fig, ax= plt.subplots(figsize=(7,7), facecolor="grey") #Set up your figure
    ax.axis("equal") # Equal aspect ratio ensures the pie chart is circular
    ax.pie(data,labels=labels, explode=explode, autopct ="%1.1f%%", colors=colors, shadow=True)#Create the pie chart based on interested parameters
    ax.legend(labels=labels, loc=0); #Giving name to the legend and location of the legend on the figure."0" stands for the best postiion on the figure 
    ax.set_title(title, fontsize = 15);# Set the title name and the size

Data Limitations¶

Some of the information was incorrect, such as patients who were under age 0. Additional information like employment, occupation, health insurance, etc. might be helpful in determining why patients cancel or attend appointments.

Conclusions¶

The first pie chart presented reveals that females attended the appointments more than males. The females attended almost twice as much as the males.

The second pie chart analysed the ratio of hypertensive patients to non-hypertensive patients who attened the appointment. Therefore, we can assume that lesser percentage of patients with hypertension attended the appointment while a larger percentage of patients with hypertension missed the appointment.

The histogram provided depicts which age group attended the appointment the most. Therefore, we can assume based on the histogram that patients within the age group 0-10 years attended the most. Interestingly, the histogram reveals a gradual increase in attendance for young and middle age groups of patients (0-20 years and 20-40 years respectively), however there was a sharp decline in attendance for the old age groups (50 years).
